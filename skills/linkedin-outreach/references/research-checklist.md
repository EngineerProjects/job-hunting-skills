# Research Checklist — Company & Contact

Used in Step 4 of linkedin-outreach: find 1-2 concrete anchors before drafting the message.
Work through the sources in order. Stop as soon as you have 2 solid anchors.

---

## Signal sources (check in this order)

### 1. LinkedIn company page — recent posts
```
https://www.linkedin.com/company/[slug]/posts/
```
Look for:
- [ ] Funding announcement (Series A/B/C, seed round)
- [ ] Product launch or major feature release
- [ ] New office / expansion
- [ ] Hiring milestone ("We're growing the team")
- [ ] Technical blog post or engineering article

**Anchor quality check:** is it from the last 90 days? Is it specific enough to name in one sentence?

---

### 2. LinkedIn job postings
```
https://www.linkedin.com/company/[slug]/jobs/
```
Look for:
- [ ] Roles that overlap with your profile (data, ML, engineering)
- [ ] Number of open positions (signals team growth)
- [ ] Stack listed in the JD (confirms your relevance)
- [ ] Seniority level (signals what they need)

**Anchor use:** "I saw you're actively hiring Senior Data Engineers in Paris" is a low-effort anchor that still shows you paid attention.

---

### 3. Contact's own LinkedIn activity
```
https://www.linkedin.com/in/[contact-handle]/recent-activity/
```
Look for:
- [ ] Post they wrote in the last 30 days (topic, opinion, something they built)
- [ ] Article they shared with a comment
- [ ] Conference talk or event they mentioned

**Best anchor type:** reference something they personally published, not just company news.
Example: "Your post on [technical topic] last week raised a point I've been thinking about too."

---

### 4. Public news (use WebSearch if needed)
Queries to try:
- `[company name] funding 2025 2026`
- `[company name] techcrunch OR sifted OR les echos`
- `[company name] raises series`
- `[company name] launch product`

Look for:
- [ ] Funding news (TechCrunch, Sifted, Les Echos, Maddyness for French startups)
- [ ] Acquisition or partnership
- [ ] Executive hire (new CTO, CPO — signals strategic shift)
- [ ] Press coverage of product or team

---

### 5. GitHub / technical presence (optional, for technical contacts)
```
https://github.com/[company-handle]
```
Look for:
- [ ] Recent open-source repos or contributions
- [ ] Tech stack actually used (confirms JD claims)
- [ ] Any public project you can genuinely reference

---

## Anchor scoring

Before using an anchor in the message, score it:

| Criteria | Bad anchor | Good anchor |
|---|---|---|
| Specificity | "you're a growing company" | "your Series B in April" |
| Recency | 12 months ago | last 90 days |
| Relevance to you | generic company news | connects to your specific stack or domain |
| Verifiable | vague | something they publicly announced |

**Min score to use**: at least 2/4 criteria met. If you can't find one good anchor, say so in the dossier and use the job posting as fallback.

---

## Fallback anchor (when nothing concrete is found)

If no recent signal exists, fall back to the job posting itself:
```
"I came across your [Role] posting and the [specific requirement from JD] caught my attention."
```

This is weaker but still specific. Never use "I admire your company's work" or any generic variant — those are worse than no anchor at all.

---

## Output format for Step 4

After research, summarize:

```
Company: [Name]
Research date: [date]

Anchor 1: [one sentence, specific, with source]
  Source: [LinkedIn post URL / news article URL / job posting URL]
  Recency: [X days ago]
  Relevance: [why it connects to your profile]

Anchor 2: [one sentence, specific, with source] — or "none found"

Fallback: [job posting title and one specific detail from JD]
```
